Traditional Clothes in Kurdistan

Traditional clothes is a big part of the Kurdish culture. Kurdish people wear their traditional clothing in their daily life, during celebrations, and during holidays. There is even a national cultural clothes day where everyone in Kurdistan wears their traditional clothes. That is on March 10th. On this day all the kurds perform their work and gathering in Kurdish clothes. It is not only the Kurds in Kurdistan who have their traditonal clothes and culutre. There is also the Assyrian and Yazidis among others. The next day when most of the Kurdish populaiton in Kurdistan and all over the world wear Kurdish clothes is on Newroz, which is the Kurdish New Years, on March 21st. The month of March is the most celebrated in Kurdistan mainly becuase of Newroz. People go out with their families to picnic sites wearing their traditional clothes and playing and dancing on the tunes of Kurdish music.

Kurdish clothes have more vibrant colors for women and more or less neutral colors for men. Women's Kurdish clothes have multiple lays and underlayers and they chnage based on city/tribe/religion. Men's clothing also change depending on city/tribe/religion, but they might not have as many layers similar to women's cultural clothes.
