Let's Go to Kurdistan

Kurdistan is a region in the Middle East. Kurdistan is a region divided by the borders of four countries. Kurds call the four parts by their cardinal directions: Bakur (North), Bashur (South), Rojava (West), Rojhelat (East). Bakur is in southeastern Turkey. I am from Bashur, which lies in northern Iraq. Rojava is in northern Syria. The last part, Rojhalat is in northwestern Iran. All four parts of Kurdistan have amazing nature and a lot of historical cites due to the diverse geographical location but also the historical context and ethno-religious diversity. I have lived all my life in Bashur and thus I would like to show you some of the thing you might want to do if you are to visit Kurdistan's Bashur.

During my time there I used to go on various hiking and camping trips and would like to share some pictures of some the various places I have been to in Bashur. Bashur has beautiful mountanous areas, but that is not all that it offers, it also has rivers, waterfalls, and caves among others. On your hikes, tubing, rafting, and camping trips, you will pass by villages and see wild and domestic animals wondering around and enjoying nature. We also hold parties in the mountains and by the rivers some of those are parties plus camping as the parties go on for two days. Nature is not the only think Kurdistan's Bashur offers. Bashur have developed cities that are filled with tall buildings, malls, resturants, and similar places. It also has various historical cities within and around each of the cities. Bashur also has culutral events and holidays during which you might want to visit such as the Kurdish New Year on March 21st.
