Let's Go to Kurdistan

Kurdistan is a region in the Middle East. Kurdistan is a region divided by the borders of four countries. Kurds call the four parts by their cardinal directions: Bakur (North), Bashur (South), Rojava (West), Rojhelat (East). Bakur is in southeastern Turkey. I am from Bashur, which lies in northern Iraq. Rojava is in northern Syria. The last part, Rojhalat is in northwestern Iran. All four parts of Kurdistan have amazing nature and a lot of historical cites due to the diverse geographical location but also the historical context and ethno-religious diversity. I have lived all my life in Bashur and thus I would like to show you some of the thing you might want to do if you are to visit Kurdistan's Bashur.

Before talking about what to do, let me walk you through the geoghraphy of Kurdistan Bashur. In Bashur, there are four governorates: Duhok, Erbil/Hawler, Sulaymaniyah/Slemani, and Halabja. Each of these governorates have a capital city carrying the same name as the governorate.

Duhok

I am from Duhok city, in Duhok governorate. Duhok city is a valley area between two moutnain chains. Duhok is known for its beautiful nature and hospitable people. As a tourist in Duhok, you might want to take a hike in one of the closeby mountians that can be a 5 to 10 minutes walk from your hotel depending on where you stay. Duhok also has a dam which can be viewed from the mountian and can be driven to from almost anywhere in Duhok within 30 minutes. If you are not a nature or hiking person. There is always museums and downtown to see the bazar and enjoy food, traditional goods, and music. There are a varity of food options and bars.


Erbil

Erbil is the capital of Kurdistan Bashur and the biggest city in Bashur. I lived in Erbil for 5 years and I would say if you like the big city life, Erbil is the place to go. Erbil is a more dry land with no mountians or much of forst surrounding it. It is an industrial and fast growning city. Erbil is known for its tall building, beautiful historical cites such as the Hawler Citadel, and its vibrance especially in Ainkawa, downtown, and Bazar areas. In Erbil, you can visit the different parks and see the mounaments in each park. You can visit downtowna and see the historical Hawler citadel while shoping from your local stores and getting traditional sourveniurs. Erbil has an international community due to the growing buinesses, international organizations, and local and international companies. There are a varity of international resturants, bars, and clubs. There are also small communities that organizes sport events, parties, and more. See Visit Erbil's Instagram page for more pcitures and outside of the city adventures.

Sulaymaniyah

Sulaymaniyah is similar in nature to Duhok and similar in size and advancement to Erbil. It is a mix of both world. I ahve only visited Sulaymaniyah a few times, but I can tell that if you like nautre and enjoy the vibes of a big city, that is where you need to be. Sulaymaniayh has a vibrant cultural ambience in it bazars with more nature. In Sulaymaniyah, you might want to also visit the mountians in the city and see the city view from above. You can enjoy a stroll in the busy bazar area or sit in a cafe with friends and disucss pressing matters.

Halabja

Last but not least, Halabja. I have been to Halabja one time. It is the smallest of the goernorates and the cities. It is also the most recent to become a governorate. Halabja is the city that suffered from a chemical attack during Sadam Hussien's presidency and it is most known for its Memorial Monoumnet to commemorate the martyrs.

Outdoors

Bashur has beautiful mountanous areas, but that is not all that it offers, it also has rivers, waterfalls, and caves among others. On your hikes, tubing, rafting, and camping trips, you will pass by villages and see wild and domestic animals wondering around and enjoying nature. We also hold parties in the mountains and by the rivers some of those are parties plus camping as the parties go on for two days. Nature is not the only think Kurdistan's Bashur offers. Bashur have developed cities that are filled with tall buildings, malls, resturants, and similar places. It also has various historical cities within and around each of the cities. Bashur also has culutral events and holidays during which you might want to visit such as the Kurdish New Year on March 21st.
